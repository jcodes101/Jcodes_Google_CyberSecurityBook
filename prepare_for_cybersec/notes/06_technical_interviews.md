# What are technical interviews?

After you apply for a cybersecurity role and a recruiter shows interest, you may go through several steps: an **introductory** interview, a **second** interview with a **hiring manager**, a **panel** interview, and sometimes a **technical interview**.

The main difference with a **technical interview** is the focus on **knowledge of specific tools** and **concepts** you will use on the job—not only culture fit or general experience stories.

---

## Python

**Python** is widely used in security and may come up in a technical interview. Be ready to describe **basic** Python knowledge: why it is popular (**readable syntax**), its **libraries** and **integrations**, and how it supports **automation** (parsing logs, orchestrating tasks, small tools).

You might be asked to **whiteboard pseudocode** in Python. Using **correct terminology** (functions, loops, data structures, libraries you have actually used) signals solid grounding.

For deeper study notes in this repo, see **`automate_cybersec_python/`**.

## General techniques

Interviewers often probe **foundational security** knowledge:

- **Security frameworks** — structured guidance for managing **risk** to data and privacy. Mentioning **NIST** frameworks—for example the **NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)**—shows you can connect controls to business risk.  
- **Network security** — practices that protect **network infrastructure** from **unauthorized access** and abuse.

**Review** topics from your certificate program so explanations stay crisp under pressure.

### Slow down and structure your answer

It can help to **write the question down** before you answer. Many technical prompts have **multiple parts**; rushing can make you miss a required detail. Restating the question confirms understanding and gives you a **checklist** while you respond.

---

## Possible technical interview questions

Every interview differs by company and interviewer. Here are **sample** prompts and concise reference answers:

### What is the TCP/IP model?

The **TCP/IP model** is a framework used to visualize how data is **organized** and **transmitted** across a network.

### What is the OSI model?

The **OSI model** (**open systems interconnection** model) is a standardized description of the **seven layers** computers use to communicate and send data over a network.

### What are SIEM tools and what are they used for?

**SIEM** (**security information and event management**) tools help security professionals **collect**, **detect**, **analyze**, and investigate **threats**, **risks**, and **vulnerabilities** using centralized log and event data.

---

## Key takeaways

- **Technical interviews** emphasize **tools** and **concepts**—prepare examples from coursework, labs, and personal projects.  
- Be ready to discuss **Python** basics and **whiteboard** simple logic.  
- Refresh **frameworks** (e.g., **NIST CSF**), **network security**, and other core modules from the program.  
- Use **structured** answering (write down multi-part questions) to avoid missing requirements.  
- Even without prior security employment, you can demonstrate **what you learned** in the Google Cybersecurity Certificate and your motivation to apply it on the job.
