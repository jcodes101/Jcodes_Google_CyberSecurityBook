# Using visuals to communicate effectively

Security work protects **reputation** and **financial** health. Fast, effective response often depends on **clear communication** among busy **stakeholders**.

Stakeholders usually juggle many priorities. **Visuals** can surface what matters, speed understanding, and help leaders **act** on risks to the organization’s **security posture**.

---

## Visual dashboards

A **visual dashboard** displays multiple kinds of data **in one place** so viewers can absorb the story quickly. Dashboards are especially strong when the narrative is built from **numbers** and **trends** (counts, rates, comparisons over time).

Complexity can range from a **single chart** to **many** charts, graphs, and tables. Choose the layout that matches the **question** you are answering and the **decision** you need.

**Accuracy and care** matter: misleading scales, missing context, or sloppy labels can create the wrong impression—double-check data and definitions before you share.

**Pro tip:** Spreadsheet tools such as **Google Sheets** or **Apache OpenOffice Calc** can support simple dashboards and charts for stakeholder updates.

---

## When to use visual communication

Security is a **team sport**; how you communicate with colleagues matters (see also note **04**).

Sometimes a short **email** is enough. Other times an **attachment** adds detail. A **phone call** can unblock decisions quickly when async chat or email is too slow.

**Visuals** shine when the audience must grasp **patterns in quantitative data** quickly.

### Example: phishing click audit

Your supervisor asks for results from an **internal audit** across **five departments**: how many **phishing** emails employees **clicked** over the **last five months**.

A **chart** or **graph** usually communicates that story faster than a long prose summary: departments on one axis, clicks over time as series or totals as bars—whatever makes the comparison honest and obvious.

---

## Key takeaways

- Stakeholders are **busy**; **clarity** and **brevity** help everyone.  
- **Visual dashboards** consolidate metrics so decision-makers see **actionable** risk and performance signals.  
- Use visuals when the message is **numeric**, **comparative**, or **trend-based**—and always prioritize **accurate** representation of the underlying data.
