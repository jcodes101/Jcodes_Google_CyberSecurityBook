# Information vs intelligence (and OSINT)

The terms **information** and **intelligence** are often used interchangeably, but they differ in focus and objective.

---

## Information vs intelligence

- **Information**: a collection of raw data or facts about a subject
- **Intelligence**: analysis of information that produces knowledge or insights to support decision-making

Example: New information is released about an operating system (OS) update used on workstations. After researching multiple cybersecurity news sources, you find new threats linked to the update. Analyzing and integrating that information creates intelligence that can guide a decision about whether and when to install the update.

In other words, intelligence is derived from information through **analysis**, **interpretation**, and **integration**.

---

## Intelligence improves decision-making (in security)

Organizations use information to gain insights and improve decision-making. In security, **open-source information** can be analyzed to produce intelligence about threats and vulnerabilities that might pose risk.

**OSINT** (open-source intelligence) plays a significant role in **information security (InfoSec)**—keeping data in all states away from unauthorized users.

Example: An InfoSec team monitors forums and hacker communities for discussions about emerging vulnerabilities. If a weakness is reported in software the organization uses, the team can assess risk, prioritize patching, and implement safeguards to reduce the chance of an attack.

Ways OSINT can be used to generate intelligence:

- provide insights into cyber attacks
- detect potential data exposures
- evaluate existing defenses
- identify unknown vulnerabilities

Collecting intelligence can also support **vulnerability management**, helping teams make data-driven decisions to improve defenses.

---

## OSINT tools (examples)

Because there is a huge amount of open-source information online, tools can help you find and organize relevant information.

Example tools and resources:

- **VirusTotal**: analyzes suspicious files, domains, URLs, and IP addresses for malicious content
- **MITRE ATT&CK®**: a knowledge base of adversary tactics and techniques based on real-world observations
- **OSINT Framework**: a web-based interface that helps find OSINT tools for many sources and platforms
- **Have I Been Pwned**: searches for breached email accounts

Information can come from many sources (search engines, social media, discussion boards, blogs, etc.). It’s important to research and validate sources relevant to the problem you’re solving.

---

## Key takeaways

- **Information** is raw facts; **intelligence** is analyzed information used for decisions.
- **OSINT** supports evidence-based security decisions that can help prevent attacks.
- Strong searching and research skills matter because of how much information is available.
- Familiarity with common OSINT tools/resources can speed up intelligence gathering.
